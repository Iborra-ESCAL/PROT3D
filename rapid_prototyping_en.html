1. Understanding Rapid Prototyping

Rapid Prototyping (RP) refers to a group of techniques used to quickly fabricate a scale model of a physical part using three-dimensional Computer-Aided Design (CAD) data (Chua et al., 2010). It has shifted the paradigm from subtractive to additive manufacturing.

2. The RepRap Revolution & Arduino

The RepRap Project (Replicating Rapid Prototyper) was founded in 2005 by Dr. Adrian Bowyer at the University of Bath. Its goal was to create a self-replicating machine, democratizing manufacturing (Bowyer, 2011).

The Arduino Catalyst

Arduino provided the "brain" for these machines. Simple, low-cost microcontrollers like the Mega 2560 allowed developers to control stepper motors and thermal heaters without proprietary software (Banzi & Shiloh, 2014).

FDM vs FFF

While identical in process, FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) is a trademarked term by Stratasys. The open-source community coined FFF (Fused Filament Fabrication) to avoid legal constraints (Gibson et al., 2015).

G-Code optimization is the bridge between design and hardware. Parameters like layer height, head speed, and flow rate dictate structural integrity and surface finish (Prusa, 2019).
